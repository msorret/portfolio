Building UX & UI Design Courses at Codecademy

Developing content for courses in user research, interaction design, and visual design for E-learning platform Codecademy

My Role

In May of 2022, I was hired by Codecademy as a freelance Content Contributor, to assist in the development of their new UX & UI design courses. Over the course of a year and a half, I worked on three production cycles, developing content for the courses Learn User Research: Generative, Learn Interaction Design, and Learn Visual Design.

In this role, I wrote lessons, learning standards, projects, quizzes, and practice content based on objectives and a course plan designed by the Lead Curriculum Designer. I also designed and prototypes a number of applets, working with the applet developer to produce them for specific lessons. All content items went through a minimum of 2 rounds of review, by both the Lead Curriculum Designer and the team's Subject Matter Expert.

During these productions, I spent 10-15 hours a week developing the content, in addition to my full time teaching position.


Beginner Course: Learn User Research: Generative

For this production, I developed two articles and one project: How to Conduct User Interviews, How to Analyze Qualitative Data, and Connect Best: User Interview. These content items fit into the total course based on the syllabus below.

The articles covered how to conduct effective user interviews and how to analyze the data from those user interviews. For the article How to Conduct User Interviews, I also designed a simple applet to allow users to practice running a mock interview. I built a high-fidelity prototype in Figma worked with an in-house applet developer to develop the applet in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Take a look at the applet using this link.

The project prompted users to use what they learned to conduct user interviews based on the following problem statement:

In the United States, adults report that it is difficult to meet people who share similar hobbies and interests. They want to befriend like-minded people, but they don't know where to start. Additionally, it can be difficult to maintain friendships with people they don't see on a regular basis. This problem is important because they want to enjoy good company in their free time.

In this project, users had to:

  • Develop a Research Plan
  • Prepare an Interview Discussion Guide
  • Conduct User Interviews
  • Analyze Data using an Affinity Map


I developed a template, as well as completed this project using the platform Miro, to provide users with an answer key.

See the project solution here.

Intermediate Course: Learn Interaction Design

For this production, I developed the Information Architecture and Navigation module, which included the Information Architecture Lesson, the Information Architecture and Navigation Quiz, and the Information Architecture: Food Foundations Project.

The lesson covered the basic principles of information architecture and navigation, including how to run content audits and develop a site map. The quiz tested users on the concepts and skills covered in the lesson.

The project prompted users to use what they learned to run a content audit and develop a site map for the following problem statement.

You and your team have just received a generous grant to find solutions for humanitarian issues in your community by a group of donors. The donors are primarily focused on addressing food insecurity, defined as a lack of consistent access to enough food for every person in a household to live an active, healthy life. More than 34 million people experience food insecurity in the United States.

You have been tasked to design and develop a new website to spread awareness or address food insecurity in some way. The first step of your goal is to design and present the information architecture.

In this project, users had to:

  • Conduct a Content Audit
  • Develop a Site Map Diagram


I developed a template, as well as completed this project using the platform Miro, to provide users with an answer key. See the project solution here.
